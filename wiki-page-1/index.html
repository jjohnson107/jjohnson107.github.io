Crown shyness (also canopy disengagement,IL) canopy shyness,I] or inter-crown spacing(3]) is a phenomenon observed in some tree species, in which the crowns of fully stocked trees do not touch each other, forming a canopy with channel-like gaps. +S The phenomenon is most prevalent among trees of the same species, but also occurs between trees of different species. ]l7 There exist many hypotheses as to why crown shyness is an adaptive behavior, and research suggests that it might inhibit spread of leaf-eating insect larvae.
